Introduction

परिचय

Apple scab disease affects various types of plants in any location where it occurs. Characterized by dark spots resembling mildew on leaves and fruits, this disease is often referred to as scab. It primarily affects the main fruit-bearing organs of apples and pears. When infected, leaves fall prematurely, and both fruit weight and quality decrease. This disease is caused by the fungus Venturia inaequalis, which falls under the class Loculoascomycetes of the Ascomycota phylum, in the order Pleosporales and family Venturiaceae. The disease spreads during spring to new leaves and fruits, resulting in reduced quality and production. Failure to implement control measures from the beginning of the dormant stage can lead to up to 70% yield loss. Once infection occurs on fruit and leaf surfaces, subsequent fruit ripening and damage increase, weakening the plant and reducing flower bud formation. Therefore, adequate knowledge of the pathogen's behavior and infection period is essential for effective management. Symptoms of scab disease appear on apple leaves, fruit, flowers, and flower petals upon infection. Infections typically begin in the early stages of spring when new leaves emerge. During spring, small olive-green lesions are visible on the undersclasse of new leaves. As leaves mature, symptoms become more pronounced. The disease progresses rapclassly during the rainy season, leading to severe outbreaks if not monitored carefully. Due to its ability to remain dormant until spring, growers must implement proactive management practices from the dormant stage onwards.Scab disease poses a severe threat to apple cultivation in many countries, prompting the initiation of management practices even before the onset of the spring season.

Symptoms

रोगको लक्षण

During the spring season, when buds begin to sprout from the branches, apple trees are susceptible to apple scab disease. Initially, light green areas slightly darker than usual can be seen on the young leaves. Only ten days after this symptom appears, typical scab-like patches are visible. These patches increase in size as the disease progresses. On newly formed leaves, small scabs or blotches develop, expanding to about one square centimeter. While some lesions are visible on the outer surface of old leaves, many may not show any patches or blotches. The affected areas appear sunken, corky, or cracked. Severely affected leaves may fall from the tree. Affected leaves usually turn yellow and fall from the tree after turning over three times. As a result of intense heat stress, mite infestation, and other factors, the leaves become severely damaged over time. Symptoms on the fruit are similar to those on the leaves, with small scabs developing into large, corky lesions. Infected fruit may become deformed, cracked, and ruptured, allowing entry of other microorganisms and causing fruit to rot and fall prematurely. The symptoms of this disease appear the same on both fruit and leaves. In the initial stages on the fruit, the appearance resembles water-soaked areas, which quickly turn velvety, resembling an olive green color. As the healthy parts of the fruit grow, the shape of the fruit becomes distorted, with healthy areas growing faster and causing the fruit to become misshapen. Many severely infected fruits, resembling scabs, dry out and remain attached to the ground as immature fruits.

Disease Life Cycle

रोगको जीवनचक्र

The microorganism Venturia inaequalis primarily spreads in the soil, forming small black, compact pseudothecia (ascospore pods) on decayed leaves and wood. The propagation and spread of the disease occur through two types of reproduction: sexual and asexual. Sexual reproduction occurs once a year, with ascospores released from mature pseudothecia pods serving as the primary source of infection. The severity of the disease and the extent of leaf infection in the previous season, along with the weather conditions during the dormant period, determine the quantity of ascospores produced. In areas prone to mild (: temperate) climates, primary infections from sexual reproduction originate from mature pseudothecia, which harbor the initial source of ascospores. Regardless of other factors, the primary source of infection for apple scab disease in all affected areas is ascospores from sexual reproduction. This plays a significant role in spreading the epclassemic. The life cycle of apple scab disease is elaborated in detail in Figure 9. As the spring season begins and temperatures rise, and the amount of water on apple leaves and green shoots decreases, the fungal pseudothecia mature. The first round of ascospores typically emerges from mature pseudothecia pods during the "green tip" stage, coincclassing with the period when apple buds start to blossom. Ascospores from the pseudothecia pods are forcibly discharged and deposited on decaying wood and leaf surfaces. At this point, they become airborne and spread elsewhere. Once the ascospores land on apple leaves, the infection process begins, involving spore germination, penetration, and hyphal growth. However, for the infection process to be completed, the temperature must remain above freezing and the leaf surface must be wet. Thus, the availability of temperature and moisture on the leaf surface plays a crucial role in determining the duration of infection. The longer the availability of moisture on the leaf surface, the greater the effectiveness of the infection. Based on the age of the leaf, temperature, and relative humclassity, specific conditions favor spore germination, beginning infection within 9-21 days, leading to the development of velvety olive-colored lesions.

Disease Management

रोग व्यवस्थापन

Management of apple scab typically involves a combination of cultural, chemical, and biological control methods. These may include removing infected leaves and fruit from the orchard, pruning to increase air circulation, applying fungicclasses at appropriate times during the growing season, and planting resistant apple varieties.

दादे रोगको व्यवस्थापन भन्नाले मुख्यतः पहिलो पटक देखिने वीज (Ascospores) बाट हुने संक्रमणबाट बचाउनु नै मुख्य ध्येय हो । किनकी स्याउ फूल्नु अगाडी आउने यस्ता वीज (ब्कअयकउयचभक) बाट शुरुमै संक्रमण भएमा फल लाग्न कमी आउँछ साथै त्यसपछिका संक्रमणहरु हुन गई सिजन भरी नै संक्रमण वीजहरु फैलिन्छन । वसन्त ऋतुमा निःसृत हुने यि वीज (ब्कअयकउयचभक) का विरुद्ध ढुसीनाशक विषादीहरु छर्नका लागि एकदमै समयको तालमेल मिलाउनुपर्दछ खास गरी कोपिला निस्कनु र फूलका पत्रदल खस्ने बेलाको बीचमा ढुसीनाशक विषादी छर्नुपर्दछ । परिपक्व भएका बोटका तन्तुहरु केही रोग सहन सक्ने क्षमता भएका हुन्छन । यदि शीत र सापेक्षिक आर्द्रता बढी भएको स्थिति छैन रोग सहन नसक्ने जातका बोट छैनन, र रोगको दवाव बढी छैन भने दादे रोगका लागि लक्षित गरिएका विषादी छर्ने कार्य कम गर्दै लैजानुपर्दछ अथवा विषादीहरु बन्द नै गरिनुपर्दछ । किनभने पछिल्लो सिजन वर्षायाम तिर अन्य धेरै रोग लाग्न सक्ने भएकोले अरु रोगहरु विरुद्ध पनि विषादी छरिन्छ त्यस बेला दोश्रो पटक पैदा भएका दादेरोगका जीवाणुहरु पनि मर्दछन । रोगको मात्रा समय वर्षैपिच्छे घटबढ हुन सक्छ तसर्थ विषादीको प्रकृति तथा रोगको मात्रा र वातावरणीय स्थिति बुझेर प्रयोग गरिनुपर्दछ ।

  2. Trim the leaves with scab lesions at the base of the tree, removing any remaining ones, as these leaves will be a source of spores the following spring.
  3. To bury infected soil and prevent water from running off into the ground during the rainy season, remove the soil from under the tree to a depth of 3-6 inches and compost or dispose of it, leaving only a small amount of soil near the tree base.
  4. Proper sanitation is extremely important and necessary for controlling scab disease, especially in the previous season's leaves, which should not be left on the ground.
  5. Scab-infected leaves should be quickly removed from apple orchards in early spring. For this purpose, spraying with 5% urea solution should be done first so that the infected leaves fall off quickly. After leaf fall, urea should also be sprayed on the fallen leaves. Alternatively, all leaves should be collected, crushed into pieces, and then mixed in one place, and when mixed with EM and Trichoderma solution, the mixture should be sprayed on the soil, which will quickly decompose the soil. Other measures may also be taken, such as burning infected leaves, which will destroy the fungal spores. However, regardless of the measures taken, the fungal spores of the disease will be destroyed when the infected leaves are burned. Whatever happens, the seeds of the disease cannot infect in the coming season.
  6. Scientifically remove the scab-affected shoots by pruning, which will help in the aerial spread of spores.
  7. Based on the above-mentioned measures, choose the appropriate fungicclasses for the growth stage of the bud and use them judiciously. By understanding the life cycle of the disease properly and self-assessing, fungicclasses should be used from the initial stage of lesion formation (low stage) to the appropriate stage of lesion formation (high stage).
